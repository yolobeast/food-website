Yapping About Yogurt

       How is yogurt made?

In Canada it is required that yogurt is made with live cultures. Some yogurt are heat-treated meaning final product has no active culture. Yogurts are pasteurized, homogenized, heated, cooled and inoculated before going for incubation. Incubation is  when the yogurt mixture is incubated for 4-5 hours at 105-115°F. This is the optimal temperature for the bacteria used as they would be killed about 130° as well they do not grow below 98°F. Yogurt becomes firm when a pH of 4.6 is reached. The more the mixture is incubated the more acidity made the more sour the taste. To end fermentation after the pH is reached yogurt is cooled to around 45°F .​

Is this good for me?

  • Nutrient-rich, filled with calcium, potassium, and protein
  • Many are probiotics as well as Vitamin D enriched
  • Probiotics are live microorganisms that develop symbiotic relationships with their host when consumed in reasonable amounts
  • Probiotics may promote immune function as it helps maintain healthy gut bacteria
  • Probiotics may improve mental health and protect against cognitive impairment
  • May lower the risk of some chronic diseases such as diabetes and cancer  ​

 How do I preserve this?

1-2 months in a freezer that is -18 degrees Celsius or lower
1-2 weeks in a fridge that is 4 degrees Celsius or lower

                Food Safety 

  • For Freshness and Quality, it should be consumed within 1-2 weeks if refrigerated and 1-2 months if frozen
  • Shelf-life about 10-21 days, for liquid yogurt about 4-10 days
  • It should be stored in refrigerated environment of about 40°F
  • If there are visible signs of microbial growth and off-odours should be discarded immediately
  • Yogurt acidity acts as bacterial growth barrier
An example of microbial growth on a yogurt cup

Proper Disposal 
​Spoiled yogurt can be put in a green bin, composted, thrown in the regular garbage, or put into the sink and toilet. Many yogurt cups are recycling friendly, so remove the yogurt from its packaging, wash it out, and then throw it into your blue bin.
