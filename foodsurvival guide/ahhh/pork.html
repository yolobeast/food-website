Let's Ponder Some Pork

Is this good for me?

  • High protein content ~20%
  • High vitamin B1, good source of B12, B2, B6 and Niacin
  • Good source of iron, zinc and selenium'
  • For a healthier option choose lean cuts, trim away excess fat​

How do I prepare this?

You should never rinse pork! Also ensure that you cook it through, pork should never be 'medium' or 'medium rare'. Ground pork should be cooked to 160°F (71.7°C) and pork chops, steaks, and roasts need to reach a minimum internal temperature of 145°F (62.7°C).

How do I preserve this?
8-12 months in a freezer that is -18 degrees Celsius or lower
2-4 days in a fridge that is 4 degrees Celsius or lower

Food Safety

  • Raw or undercooked pork can be a source of foodborne microorganisms such as E. coli, Salmonella, S. aureus, Yersinia enterocolitica, L. monocytogenes, Trichinella spiralis
  • Do not cross-contaminate utensils and food contact surfaces
  • Always wash your hands after touching raw pork
  • Raw pork should be cooked to 160°​F (71.7°C)
  • Pork chops, steaks, roast should be cooked to 145°F (62.7°C)

Proper Disposal 

                                                                                 Spoiled Raw Pork

Raw meat can be tricky. It still has all those nasty bugs in it and will attract unwanted visitors to your house. The best way to dispose of it is to put it in a plastic bag and stick it in the freezer until your garbage day. If you live away from a city some people like to leave the meat out for scavengers. Be careful though! Those little guys will come again so make sure you do it far away from your home. Also, never, ever, put raw meat down your disposal. Any harmful bacteria will leach into your system. ​

                               Cooked Pork

Disposing of cooked pork is similar to disposing of raw pork, freeze it in a bag until garbage day and then put it out. However, don't feed it to animals as they could get sick. 

Bacon Grease

This one's easy right!? Just throw it down the drain! WRONG. Bacon grease will solidify when it cools down, so pour in in a car or cup after cooking and let it solidify over night. Then throw it away in the regular garbage or compost.
