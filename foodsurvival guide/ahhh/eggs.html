Eggcelent Egg Info

Is this good for me?

  • A large egg has about 70 calories and 6 grams of protein
  • A single egg has 13 essential nutrients
  • Egg protein contains time release energy which helps maintain blood glucose levels and makes people feel full and energized longer
  • Eggs are a source of vitamin A, D, B2, Calcium, Magnesium, and Phosphorus

How do I prepare this?

  • Make sure eggs are cooked until yolk and whites are firm
  • Scrambled eggs should not be runny
  • Dishes that contain egg should be cooked at 160°F (71.1°C), make sure a Food thermometer is used
  • Make sure pasteurized egg products are used  ​

How do I preserve this?

Fresh in shell: 3-4 weeks
Fresh out of shell: 2-4 days
​Hard cooked: 1 week

Fresh in shell: don't freeze
Fresh out of shell: 4 months
​Hard cooked: don't freeze well

2 hours if the eggs were previously refrigerated. 1 week for unwashed, farm fresh eggs. Eggs bought from a store in North America cannot be left out. 

​Food Safety 

​
  • Make sure hard-boiled eggs are used within 1 week after cooking
  • Make sure frozen eggs are used within 1 year, but do not freeze eggs in their shells, make sure the white and yolk are beaten together and then freeze the eggs
  • Make sure leftover cooked egg dishes are refrigerated and eaten within 3-4 days of being cooked
  • Make sure the egg contained products are cooled down before putting in the fridge
  • Proper egg storage affects both the quality and safety of egg
  • Raw eggs must be refrigerated and make sure the eggs shells are not cracked
  • Make sure they are stored in a clean refrigerator at 40°F (4.4°C), this helps with prevention of bacterial growth of Salmonella
  • Store them in original packaging and make sure they are used within 3 weeks of buying for best quality  ​
Egg freshness test: fill a container full of room temperature water and plop your egg in. If the egg is fresh it will lie flat on the bottom, if it's very old it will float to the top.

Proper Disposal 

Whole eggs- the yolk, whites, and shells- can all be put into your green bin and composted. If unavailable they can be thrown into your regular garbage, although they will begin to smell so try to do this right before your garbage collection day. Eggshells contain nutrients which are beneficial for plants, so some people like to crush them up and sprinkle them on lawns and houseplants to improve plant health.
