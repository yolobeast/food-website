All About that Beef

Is this good for me? 

How do I prepare this?

Do not wash or rinse beef! When cooking steak, ribs, roasts, or briskets ensure that it reaches an internal temperature of 62.8 degrees Celsius. When preparing hamburgers (Yum) ensure that the beef has reached an internal temperature of 71.1 degrees Celsius. Why is this important? Because of the way beef is prepared (through a mixing grinding process) any surface contaminants will also be on the inside, so you need to make sure the whole thing is cooked through! As with all raw meats make sure you wash your hands after touching beef and before touching any of your other food to avoid the spread of bacteria.  

How do I preserve this?
10-12 months in a freezer that is -18 degrees Celsius or lower
2-4 days in a fridge that is 4 degrees Celsius or lower

Food Safety 

The two most important things to remember when preparing any raw meet is to wash your hands before touching any other food and to ensure that you've fully cooked it through to ensure that any harmful bacteria has been destroyed by the heat. For steak ribs roasts and briskets this is 62.8 °C and for hamburgers you have to aim a little higher for 71.1 °C. ​

Proper Disposal 

                                      Spoiled Raw Beef
Raw meat can be tricky. It still has all those nasty bugs in it and will attract unwanted visitors to your house. The best way to dispose of it is to put it in a plastic bag and stick it in the freezer until your garbage day. If you live away from a city some people like to leave the meat out for scavengers. Be careful though! Those little guys will come again so make sure you do it far away from your home. Also, never, ever, put raw meat down your disposal. Any harmful bacteria will leach into your system. 

                                        Beef Grease
This one's easy right!? Just throw it down the drain! WRONG. Beef grease will solidify when it cools down, so pour in in a car or cup after cooking and let it solidify over night. Then throw it away in the regular garbage or compost.

                                     Cooked Beef
Disposing of cooked beef is similar to disposing of raw beef, freeze it in a bag until garbage day and then put it out. However, don't feed it to animals as they could get sick. Many people are tempted to just put meat in the garbage, which is alright if you have small quantities and your garbage is kept in a secure place. When beef is decomposing it goes through something called an anaerobic process which releases methane as well as some nasty bugs. You don't want that right? So freeze it before you throw it out. 

The Many Colours of Beef 

The colour of beef can tell you a lot about it. If your meat is purple then it has probably been vacuum packed and had all its oxygen taken out. That's okay! You can go ahead and eat it. Bright red meat in a store is fresh and is absolutely okay to eat. If the meat is brown, smells funny, has slime, or a tacky touch DO NOT EAT IT! It has gone bad and will make you sick.
