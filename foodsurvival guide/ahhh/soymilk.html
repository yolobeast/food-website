Say it Ain't Soy!

What is soy?

Soybean plants are part of the legume family and have been common staples in Asian diets for thousands of years. They are typically, found in North American diets as a food or food additive (soy-based products). 

Is this good for me?

  • Fortified soy beverages have just as much calcium, zinc, vitamin A and D as milk
  • High in protein, ¾ cup cooked soybeans contain as much protein as ½ cup meat or fish
  • All legumes contain no cholesterol
  • “Good fat”: monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats, includes omega-3 fatty acids
  • Excellent source of calcium and iron
  • Greatest source of isoflavones in the human diet

Dairy vs Soy milk

  • Dairy milk require the use of living animals 
  • Cow's milk has about 9 grams of protein per cup
  • Cow's milk has about 13 grams of natural sugar
​
  • Made from plants, no animals harmed 
  • Had 7 grams of protein per cup 
  • Low in sugar and lactose (however flavoured soy products have twice the amount of sugar)
  • nutritionally similar to cow's milk
